Buttery lobster tail

Description

Butter-poached lobster tails represent a pinnacle of seafood luxury, defined by an incredibly silky, "melt-in-your-mouth" texture that can only be achieved through slow, gentle cooking. By carefully lifting the meat to rest atop its shell in a technique known as "piggybacking," the lobster is presented with restaurant-style elegance while remaining perfectly positioned for a consistent butter bath.

The culinary magic occurs as the tails are submerged in an emulsion of melted butter, garlic, and bright lemon. Unlike high-heat methods like grilling or boiling which can leave seafood rubbery, poaching over low heat ensures the meat stays succulent, sweet, and saturated with rich fats. Constant basting with the fragrant garlic-lemon mixture infuses every fiber of the lobster until it reaches a tender, opaque finish.

Completed with a scatter of fresh parsley, citrus zest, and an optional touch of chili heat, the dish is typically served immediately with the remaining "liquid gold" butter sauce drizzled over the top. It is frequently paired with creamy risotto, mashed potatoes, or crusty bread to capture the decadent juices, making it a definitive choice for celebrations and fine dining at home.

Ingredients and Cookware

  • 6 lobster tails
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  • 2 tbsp parsley
  • Half tsp crushed red pepper
  • Salt and cracked black pepper

Step by step instructions

  1. Cut through the top shell and lift meat over the shell, keep attached at the base.
  2. Put a large skillet on low heat. Melt butter gently and add garlic and warm until fragrant (do not brown).
  3. Add tails into a pot (shell-side down).
  4. Spoon butter over constantly for 8 to 10 minutes until the meat is opaque and tender.
  5. Stir in lemon juice, zest, parsley and optional chilli flakes (taste the butter and adjust salt or pepper).
  6. Serve and enjoy!
