Fried steak

Description

A simple, no-nonsense method for cooking a T-bone that focuses on technique over tricks. The steak is brought ot room temperature, seasoned generously, and slowly seared to build a deep crust while keeping the inside juicy. Finishing with butter and fresh thyme add richness without overpowering the meat. Resting at the end locks everything in, giving you a tender, flavorful steak that pairs perfectly with eggs and cheese for a hearty, carnivore-style meal.

Ingredients and Cookware

  • Prime T-bone (or any cut of your choice)
  • Coarse salt
  • Olive oil
  • Pepper
  • Butter
  • Thyme
  • Stainless steel pan or Nonstick pan
  • Spoon

Step by step instructions

  1. Take the steak out of the freezer or cooler, let it rest for 20 to 25 minutes for an even sear.
  2. Pat meat with napkin until dry.
  3. add salt and pepper on both sides of the steak (use generous amount).
  4. Turn the stove to medium-low heat, wait until pan gets hot before adding steak.
  5. Add a minimal amount of olive oil into the pan.
  6. Sear "fat cap" of steak first for a minute.
  7. Sear each side of the steak for 4 minutes and 30 seconds.
  8. turn heat to low and sear the edges for 45 seconds each.
  9. Add butter and thyme to the pan and "baste" the steak.
  10. Slightly tilt pan to get a good scoop and continue until the butter is completely melted.
  11. Let the steak rest for 2 to 3 minutes, do not squeeze the steak.
  12. Cut and garnish.
  13. Serve and enjoy!
