Buttered Chicken

Description

This Butter-Basted Spatchcock Chicken with Herb-Roasted Vegetables is a masterclass in savory, one-pan roasting. By securing cubes of butter directly to the chicken with toothpicks, the meat undergoes a continuous self-basting process, ensuring every bite is incredibly succulent and rich.

As the chicken roasts on the rack above, the melting butter and savory juices drip down to "confat" a medley of potatoes, carrots, and earthy mushrooms. Seasoned with garlic and aromatic fresh rosemary, the vegetables caramelize in the golden drippings, absorbing deep, roasted flavors. In just 45 minutes, you achieve a complete gourmet feast featuring crispy, buttery chicken and tender, flavor-soaked root vegetables.

Ingredients and Cookware

  • An entire chicken
  • Salt & pepper
  • butter
  • potatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Carrots
  • Mushrooms
  • Garlic powder
  • Rosemary
  • Mayonnaise
  • Ketchup
  • Chili sauce
  • Oven

Step by step instructions

  1. Cut off the tips of the wings and split the chicken length wise.
  2. Season with black pepper and salt.
  3. Place the chicken on the oven rack.
  4. Cut the butter into cubes.
  5. Secure the cubed butter onto the chicken using wooden toothpicks and set aside.
  6. Peel and wash the potatoes and put them into a bowl.
  7. Season the potatoes with salt and pepper and add 5 tsp of olive oil.
  8. Peel 2 carrots and cut into thin slices.
  9. Place the carrots into the same bowl with potatoes.
  10. To the bowl, add chopped mushrooms.
  11. Season with garlic powder and mix well.
  12. Place the vegetables with mushrooms in the baking tray.
  13. Add a few sprigs of Rosemary.
  14. Put the tray in the oven.
  15. Just above the vegetables place the rack with chicken and butter.
  16. Bake for 45 minutes.
  17. Bingo! you're done. Serve and enjoy.
