Fried steak

Description

Master the art of the perfect home-cooked steak with this refined, butter-basted method designed for deep flavor and a flawless crust. The process begins with critical preparation: allowing the steak to rest at room temperature for up to 25 minutes ensures it cooks evenly from edge to center, while thoroughly drying the surface with a napkin removes excess moisture that would otherwise steam the meat. By starting with the fat cap, you render out natural beef fats to create a flavorful base for searing. This recipe uniquely utilizes a medium-low heat approach, providing a controlled environment that builds a rich, mahogany crust without the risk of scorching, making it especially effective for thicker cuts.

The final stages of the recipe elevate the steak to restaurant standards through the classic technique of butter-basting. As you lower the heat, the addition of butter and fresh thyme creates an aromatic liquid that you spoon repeatedly over the meat, infusing it with savory herbal notes and ensuring a succulent finish. Crucially, the process concludes with a mandatory resting period; by resisting the urge to cut or squeeze the steak immediately, you allow the internal juices to redistribute, guaranteeing that every slice remains tender and moist. This patient, detail-oriented approach turns a simple piece of meat into a sophisticated culinary experience.

Ingredients and Cookware

  • Prime T-bone (or any cut of your choice)
  • Coarse salt
  • Olive oil
  • Pepper
  • Butter
  • Thyme
  • Stainless steel pan or Nonstick pan
  • Spoon

Step by step instructions

  1. Take the steak out of the freezer or cooler, let it rest for 20 to 25 minutes for an even sear.
  2. Pat meat with napkin until dry.
  3. add salt and pepper on both sides of the steak (use generous amount).
  4. Turn the stove to medium-low heat, wait until pan gets hot before adding steak.
  5. Add a minimal amount of olive oil into the pan.
  6. Sear "fat cap" of steak first for a minute.
  7. Sear each side of the steak for 4 minutes and 30 seconds.
  8. turn heat to low and sear the edges for 45 seconds each.
  9. Add butter and thyme to the pan and "baste" the steak.
  10. Slightly tilt pan to get a good scoop and continue until the butter is completely melted.
  11. Let the steak rest for 2 to 3 minutes, do not squeeze the steak.
  12. Cut and garnish.
  13. Serve and enjoy!
